Historical Places in Cape Town

Castle of Good Hope

-Castle was Built by the DEIC between 1666 and 1679 and is the oldest existing colonial building in South Africa. -Castle of Good Hope was called a Castle because, as in the case of other Castles in Europe, in addition to being a defensive structure, it comprised a small community or town on its own. The Castle was, however, not the first fort to be built at the Cape. A quadrangular (four-pointed) fort was built after the arrival of Jan van Riebeeck in 1652 where the Grand Parade and the main Post Office are situated today.

City Hall

-City hall was built on 29 August 1900 by then-mayor of Cape Town, Thomas Ball, and it was opened to the public in 1905. The hall is mainly used for council meetings, cultural events and other significant events. In 1990, the Grand Parade and Cape Town City Hall hosted one of the most historic events in South African history. On 11 February 1990, 250 000 people gathered on the parade to witness the release of Nelson Mandela and his speech after 27 years of imprisonment on Robben Island.
